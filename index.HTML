Ukraine-Russia War

Background of Ukraine-Russia war:

  • Ukraine became an independent state in 1991 after the disintegration of the USSR. Ukraine is the largest country in Europe and has a large border with Russia. Russia has always been apprehensive of its security as major attacks on Russia have occurred from the western border, like in World War 1 and World War 2.
  • Ukraine’s desire to join NATO made Russia apprehensive of its security, and it captured Crimea in 2014. Russia also supported insurgents in the Donetsk and Luhansk regions in Eastern Ukraine.
  • The war between Russia and Ukraine has increased trade ambiguity and significantly influenced oil prices and other commodities. Despite having little trade with Russia, India suffers economically due to supply disruptions by Western nations.

Reasons for Ukraine-Russia War

  • The main reason behind the Russia-Ukraine Conflict or Why Russia and Ukraine are Fighting is because Ukraine acceded to NATO, which would go against Russia’s wishes and continue to endanger its security. The west, according to Russia, should guarantee that Ukraine will never get permission to be part of NATO.
  • Economic Factor: Ukraine has a large population, and it has substantial natural minerals and resources which also produce wheat, and vegetable oil, which are supposed to play an important role. Ukraine’s membership in the Eurasian Economic Community (EAEC), a free trade agreement that came into being in 2015, but Ukraine refused to join the agreement.
  • Joining NATO of Ukraine will result in installing missiles, a missile defence system at the mouth of Moscow.

Key events

  1. February 2022-Russian forces attacked the Ukrainian capital, Kyiv.
  2. March 2022-Russian forces took the Kherson region, including the regional capital, aiming to secure Ukraine’s coast.
  3. April 2022-A Russian missile strike on a train station in the eastern city of Kramatorsk killed at least 50 civilians.
  4. May 2022- The last Ukrainian fighters surrendered to Russian forces in Mariupol.
  5. June 2022-Russian forces withdrew from Snake Island in the Black Sea.
  6. July 2023-The last city under Ukrainian control in Luhansk.
  7. August 2022-Kyiv’s forces employed Western-supplied weapons systems.
  8. September 2022-Ukraine would recapture the eastern city of Lyman the next month.
  9. October 2022-In a blow to Moscow’s war effort, an explosion damaged the Kerch Strait Bridge linking Crimea with Russia.
  10. November 2022-Russian forces withdrew from the city of Kherson.
  11. December 2022-With little territory changing hands on the battlefield, Ukraine carried out drone attacks on military bases hundreds of miles inside Russia.
  12. January 2023-Ukrainian forces struck a building in Donetsk that was housing Russian troops.
